History

Script:

Tourism in North Korea has a long and complicated history, which reflects its political and economic situation. The origins of tourism in North Korea can be traced back to the 1950s, when the country opened its doors to visitors from the Soviet Union, China, and other socialist countries, as a way of showing its solidarity and friendship. The motivations of tourism in North Korea changed in the 1980s, when the country faced a severe economic crisis and sought to attract foreign currency and investment from Western and Asian countries, as a way of boosting its economy and image. The policies of tourism in North Korea also changed in the 2000s, when the country adopted a more flexible and pragmatic approach to tourism, allowing more private and joint ventures, more diverse and customized tours, and more access and interaction with the local people and culture.
